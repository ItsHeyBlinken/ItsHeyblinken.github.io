Make the next step easy for recruiters.

Social posts, video links, stat sheets, and academic details often live in different places. A recruiting profile organizes the information a coach needs without asking them to assemble the story.

02 · Profile essentials

Everything important, organized for the recruiting conversation.

Athlete introduction

Name, sport, graduation year, positions or event specialties, biography, goals, and prominent quick-reference metrics.

Athletics

Teams, coaches, athletic details, recent achievements, awards, and sport-specific information presented clearly.

Academics

GPA, class rank, test scores when available, honors, intended major, and extracurricular activities.

Highlight videos

Up to three or five hosted clips, grouped by configurable categories so coaches can focus on relevant skills.

Statistics

Season labels and sport-specific stat groups that adapt to the athlete instead of forcing every sport into one format.

Contact and packet

Guardian-first recruiting contact, optional athlete details, and a downloadable PDF packet in one final action area.

06 · FAQ

Recruiting profile questions, answered.

Does this replace recruiting platforms?

No. It gives you an independent, polished home base that can complement team pages, recruiting platforms, coach emails, questionnaires, and social profiles.

Can the profile work for any sport?

Yes. Positions, event specialties, quick metrics, detailed stat groups, teams, achievements, and video categories are configured around the athlete's sport.

How is a minor's privacy handled?

A guardian or approved recruiting contact is the default. Athlete phone numbers, precise locations, and other optional details are published only when the family approves them.

What counts as an included update?

One defined request may include routine copy, statistic, photo, or media-link swaps. The allowance is non-rolling; redesigns, new sections, and larger changes receive a separate quote.
